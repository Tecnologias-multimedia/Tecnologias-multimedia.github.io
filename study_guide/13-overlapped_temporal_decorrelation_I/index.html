1.1 Overlapped transforms for minimizing the distortion

Transform coding implies to split the signal into chunks, and to compute the transform of each chunk. When the output coeﬃcients are quantized, it is possible that signiﬁcative (and unpleasant) distortions may appear between the border samples of the chunks (see this the Fig 1).


Figure 1: On the top-left, three consecutive chunks of a real audio sequence. On the top-right, the reconstruction of the chunks without overlapping. On the bottom-left, the extended chunk. On the bottom-right, the reconstruction of the extended chunk. See this notebook.

One solution for the current (i-th) chunk is to use the last samples of the previous ((i − 1)-th) chunk and the ﬁrst samples of the next ((i + 1)-th) chunk for computing the transform of the current (i-th) chunk. Therefore, to avoid signal discontinuities between chunks, we can overlap them and use the following algorithm:

Encoder:

  1. Input chunks i − 1, i and i + 1, Ci−1, Ci and Ci+1.
  2. Let’s the overlapped area size o, C[: o] the ﬁrst o samples of the chunk C, and C[o :] the last o samples.
  3. Di ←DWT(Ci−1[o :]|Ci|Ci+1[: o]), where Di is the decomposition of the i-th extended chunk, ⋅|⋅ denotes the concatenation of samples operator, deﬁned by
    a|b = {a,b}, (1)

    and Ci−1[o :]|Ci|Ci+1[: o] is the i-th extended chunk.

  4. Send Di.

Decoder:

  1. Receive Di.
  2. Perform Ci−1[o :]|Ci|Ci+1[: o] ←DWT−1(Di).
  3. Output Ci.

This idea has been implemented in this notebook, and the result can be seen in the Fig. 1.
