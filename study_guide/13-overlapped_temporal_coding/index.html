1.1 Overlapped transforms for minimizing the distortion

Transform coding implies to split the signal into chunks, and to compute the transform of each chunk. When the output coeﬃcients are quantized, it is possible that signiﬁcative (and unpleasant) distortions may appear between the border frames of the chunks (see the Fig 1).


Figure 1: On the top-left, three consecutive chunks of a real mono audio sequence. On the top-right, the reconstruction of the chunks without overlapping. On the bottom-left, the extended chunk. On the bottom-right, the reconstruction of the extended chunk. See this notebook.


Figure 2: Chunks overlapping. Notice that a data is repeated in the extended chunks C0 and C1. Something similar happens with b data.


Figure 3: Structure in the DWT domain of an extended chunk. o is the number of frames to overlap between adajacent chunks. C[: o] represents the ﬁrst o framees of the chunk C and C[−o :] the last x − o frames of chunk C, where x is the number of frames in C:

One solution to avoid signal discontinuitites between chunks is to overlap the chunks (see Fig. 2). Thus, the current (i-th) chunk uses also the last frames of the previous ((i − 1)-th) chunk and the ﬁrst frames of the next ((i + 1)-th) chunk to compute the transform of the current extended (i-th) chunk (see the Fig. 3). This has been described in the following algorithm:

Encoder:

  1. For i ∈{0,1,⋯}:
    1. Input chunks Ci−1, Ci and Ci+1.
    2. Di ←DWT(Ci−1[−o :]|Ci|Ci+1[: o]), where Di is the decomposition of the i-th extended chunk, ⋅|⋅ denotes the concatenation of frames operator, deﬁned by
      a|b = {a,b}, (1)

      and Ci−1[−o :]|Ci|Ci+1[: o] is the i-th extended chunk, where o is the overlapped area size, and C[: o] the ﬁrst o frames of the chunk C, and C[−o :] the last o frames.

    3. Send Di.

Notice that we sent for each chunk 2o + x frames, where x is the number of frames/chunk.

Decoder:

  1. For i ∈{0,1,⋯}:
    1. Receive Di.
    2. Perform Ci−1[−o :]|Ci|Ci+1[: o] ←DWT−1(Di).
    3. Output Ci.

This idea has been implemented in this notebook, and the result can be seen in the Fig. 1.

1.2 Reducing the data overhead

As it can be seen in the Fig. 2, the previous algorithm sends twice the DWT coeﬃcients of the overlapped areas. To avoid this waste of bandwidth, we can implement:

Encoder:

  1. For i ∈{0,1,⋯}:
    1. Input Ci and Ci+1.
    2. Di ←DWT(Ci[o :]|Ci+1[: o]), where C[o :] the last x − o frames of the chunk C, and Ci[o :]|Ci+1[: o] is the i-th right-only extended chunk.
    3. Send Di.

Notice that now, the number of sent coeﬃcients is x.

Decoder:

  1. For i ∈{0,1,⋯}:
    1. Receive Di.
    2. Compute Ci[o :]|Ci+1[: o] ←DWT−1(Di).
    3. Output Ci−1[: o]|Ci[o :].

Notice that the only that changes between this codec and the previous one is that now, the inverse transform of the extended chunks uses the last coeﬃcients of the previous extended chunk. This idea has been checked in the notebook.
