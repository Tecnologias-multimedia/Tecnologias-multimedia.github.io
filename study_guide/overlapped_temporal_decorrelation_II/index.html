1 Description

The inverse ODWT (Overlapped DWT) uses the coeﬃcientes generated by the overlapped areas between chunks to remove the chunk discontinuities when quantization is applied to the transform coeﬃcients. In the previous milestone we use an implementation that sends twice the overlapped coeﬃcients, which deﬁnetively is a source of redundancy.

To solve this problem, we can:

Encoder:

  1. For i ∈{0,1,⋯}:
    1. Input chunks i and i + 1, Ci and Ci+1.
    2. Let’s the overlapped area size o, C[: o] the ﬁrst o samples of the chunk C, and C[o :] the result of removing the ﬁrst o samples.
    3. Di ←DWT(Ci[o :]|Ci+1[: o]), where Di is the decomposition of the i-th right-shifted chunk, ⋅|⋅ denotes the concatenation of samples operator, deﬁned by
      a|b = {a,b}, (1)

      and Ci[o :]|Ci+1[: o] is the i-th right-shifted chunk.

    4. Send Di.

Decoder:

  1. For i ∈{0,1,⋯}:
    1. Receive Di.
    2. Perform Ci[o :]|Ci+1[: o] ←DWT−1(Di).
    3. Output Ci−1[: o]|Ci[o :].

In the previous milestone we have sent the DWT coeﬃcients of the overlapped areas twice. This milestone removes this redundancy. See notebook.
