1 Description

1.1 Absolute Threshold of Humman Hearing

Psychoacoustics has determined that the HAS (Human Auditory System) has a sensitivity (threshold of human hearing) that depends on the frequency of the sound. This basically means that some subbands can be quantized with a larger quantization step than others without a noticeable increase of the quantization noise.


Figure 1: Absolute threshold of humman hearing.

A good approximation of the ToHH (Threshold of Humman Hearing) for a 20-years old person can be obtained with [1]

T(f)[dB] = 3.64(f[kHz])−0.8 − 6.5ef[kHz]−3.3)2 + 10−3(f[kHz])4. (1)

This equation has been plotted in the Fig. 1.

1.2 DWT subbands and quantization steps

The number of DWT subbands

Nsb = Nlevels + 1 (2)

where Nlevels is the number of levels of the DWT. Except for the LNlevels subband, it holds that

W(Hs) = 1 2W(Hs−1), (3)

being W(⋅) the bandwidth of the corresponding subband s. Therefore, considering that the bandwidth of the audio signal is 22050 Hz, the bandwidth B(H1) of the H1 subband is 11025 Hz, B(H2) = 22025∕4, and so on. It holds that

B(LNlevels) = B(HNlevels). (4)
